# 01. Product Brief

## Product Name

Tenbagger Check

Korean name: 텐버거 체크

## One-line Description

관심 있는 종목이 장기 성장 후보인지, 경험 많은 투자자들이 보는 핵심 기준으로 스스로 점검할 수 있게 도와주는 교육형 분석 도구.

## Product Positioning

Tenbagger Check is not a stock recommendation service.

It is an educational self-check tool that helps beginner investors think through a stock idea using a structured investment framework.

The app does not tell users what to buy or sell.

Instead, it helps users ask better questions before making their own investment decisions.

## Core Product Direction

The first version should not simply ask vague questions like:

- Is the market growing?
- Is the company strong?
- Is the financial condition good?

Beginner investors often cannot answer these questions correctly because they do not know what evidence to look for.

Instead, the app should guide users through the kinds of factors that experienced investors, analysts, and long-term growth investors often consider.

The app should teach users what to check, not just ask them to score a stock.

## Target Users

This product is for:

- Beginner investors who are interested in finding long-term growth candidates.
- Individual investors who hear about stocks from YouTube, blogs, news, or social media and want to check the idea before acting.
- People who want to avoid emotional buying based on hype or FOMO.
- Users who want a simple framework for thinking about potential multi-bagger stocks.
- Investors who do not yet know how to evaluate market size, growth evidence, valuation risk, or business quality.

## User Problem

Many individual investors want to find the next big growth stock, but they often lack a structured way to evaluate ideas.

They may hear that a stock is related to AI, semiconductors, robotics, batteries, space, defense, or another exciting theme.

However, they often do not know how to separate:

- A strong long-term business
- A temporary theme stock
- A company already priced for perfection
- A risky story with weak fundamentals
- A real growth candidate that deserves deeper research

Common user problems include:

- Buying based on hype without checking evidence.
- Confusing a hot theme with a strong company.
- Ignoring valuation and entry risk.
- Looking only at upside stories.
- Not knowing what analysts or experienced investors actually check.
- Not having a repeatable framework for reviewing stock ideas.
- Not defining what would prove their investment idea wrong.

## Core Value

The app helps users pause before making an emotional investment decision.

It gives users a structured framework to review a stock idea through seven lenses:

1. Market Opportunity
2. Growth Evidence
3. Profit Leverage
4. Competitive Advantage
5. Valuation Risk
6. Catalysts
7. Risks and Disconfirming Evidence

The goal is to help users think more like experienced investors.

The app does not predict returns.

The app does not recommend stocks.

The app helps users ask better questions.

## Key Insight

A potential tenbagger is rarely just a company in a popular theme.

A stronger candidate usually needs several conditions:

- A large or rapidly expanding market
- Evidence that the company is actually growing
- A business model where profits can grow faster than revenue
- A durable competitive advantage
- A valuation that is not already too extreme
- A catalyst that could change market perception
- Clear risks that the user understands before investing

The app should make this framework easy for beginners to understand.

## MVP Goal

The first version should allow users to:

1. Enter a stock name or ticker.
2. Review seven experienced-investor-style criteria.
3. Answer guided questions for each criterion.
4. Receive a score out of 100.
5. Read a cautious interpretation of the score.
6. See strengths and risk areas.
7. Understand what they should research next.
8. See a clear notice that the result is not investment advice.

## MVP Features

### Included in Version 1

- Stock name input
- Seven guided checklist sections
- Educational explanation for each section
- Simple score calculation
- Result summary
- Strength summary
- Risk summary
- Next research suggestions
- Not-investment-advice notice

### Excluded from Version 1

- Real-time stock prices
- Financial statement data
- News data
- AI-generated investment analysis
- Buy/sell recommendations
- Price targets
- User accounts
- Saved history
- Payment
- Backend API
- Database
- Charts
- Social sharing

## Analysis Framework

The first MVP should use seven categories.

### 1. Market Opportunity

This checks whether the company operates in a market that can become much larger over time.

Users should think about:

- Whether the market is structurally growing
- Whether the growth is likely to last for years
- Whether the company has enough room to grow
- Whether the theme is more than a short-term trend

Example themes may include AI, semiconductors, energy infrastructure, robotics, cybersecurity, defense, biotech, space, or other long-term growth areas.

The app should not say these themes are automatically good investments.

### 2. Growth Evidence

This checks whether there is real evidence that the company is growing.

Users should think about:

- Revenue growth
- Earnings growth
- Order backlog
- New customers
- New products
- Overseas expansion
- Market share gains

The app should teach that a good story is not enough. There should be evidence.

### 3. Profit Leverage

This checks whether profits can grow faster than revenue.

Users should think about:

- Operating margin improvement
- Economies of scale
- High fixed-cost business structure
- Software or platform-like margins
- Manufacturing efficiency
- Pricing power

This is important because tenbagger-style returns often require major profit expansion, not just small revenue growth.

### 4. Competitive Advantage

This checks whether the company has an advantage that competitors cannot easily copy.

Users should think about:

- Technology barriers
- Patents
- Brand strength
- Cost advantage
- Network effects
- Customer lock-in
- Supply chain position
- Regulatory licenses
- Data advantage

The app should help users avoid assuming that every company in a hot industry is equally strong.

### 5. Valuation Risk

This checks whether expectations may already be reflected in the stock price.

Users should think about:

- Whether the stock has already risen sharply
- Whether valuation multiples look stretched
- Whether future growth expectations are too high
- Whether the downside could be large if expectations are missed
- Whether the user is confusing a great company with a great entry price

The app should make clear that a great company can still be a risky investment at the wrong price.

### 6. Catalysts

This checks whether there are future events that could change how the market views the company.

Users should think about:

- New product launches
- Major customer wins
- Earnings inflection
- Profit turnaround
- Industry cycle recovery
- Supply shortage
- Regulation changes
- M&A or restructuring possibilities

The app should teach that catalysts can help turn a good story into visible results.

### 7. Risks and Disconfirming Evidence

This checks whether the user understands what could make the idea wrong.

Users should think about:

- Slowing revenue growth
- Margin deterioration
- Customer loss
- Stronger competition
- Technology disruption
- Debt burden
- Regulatory problems
- Theme fading
- Overvaluation

The app should encourage users to define warning signs before investing.

## Scoring System

The first MVP should calculate a 100-point score.

Suggested weighting:

- Market Opportunity: 15 points
- Growth Evidence: 20 points
- Profit Leverage: 15 points
- Competitive Advantage: 15 points
- Valuation Risk: 15 points
- Catalysts: 10 points
- Risks and Disconfirming Evidence: 10 points

Total score: 100 points.

The scoring should be simple and understandable.

The score should not be presented as a prediction.

It should be presented as a self-check result.

## Score Interpretation

### 80 to 100

Label:

강한 검토 후보

Meaning:

The stock idea appears to satisfy many conditions that long-term growth investors often review. This does not mean the stock will rise. It means the idea may deserve deeper research.

### 60 to 79

Label:

관심 후보

Meaning:

The stock idea has some attractive elements, but important evidence may still be missing. Users should research the weak areas before making any decision.

### 40 to 59

Label:

추가 근거 필요

Meaning:

The idea may be interesting, but the current case is not strong enough. The user should be careful about relying only on theme, hype, or recent price movement.

### 0 to 39

Label:

신중 검토 필요

Meaning:

Based on the user’s answers, the stock idea does not currently look like a strong long-term growth candidate. More evidence is needed before treating it as a serious candidate.

## Result Page Requirements

The result should include:

- Stock name
- Total score
- Score label
- Cautious interpretation
- Strongest areas
- Weakest areas
- Suggested next research points
- Not-investment-advice notice

The result should avoid sounding like a buy or sell signal.

## Required Disclaimer

The app must clearly show:

"이 서비스는 투자 추천이 아닙니다. 제공되는 점수와 해석은 사용자의 자기 점검을 돕기 위한 참고 정보입니다. 모든 투자 판단과 책임은 투자자 본인에게 있습니다."

## Words to Avoid

Avoid these phrases:

- Buy
- Sell
- Strong buy
- Guaranteed return
- This stock will rise 10x
- Next confirmed tenbagger
- Target price
- Must-buy stock
- 매수 추천

- 수익 보장
- 다음 텐버거 확정
- 목표가
